# What is GEO — and why does your website need it?

Search has changed. When someone types a question into Google, ChatGPT, or Perplexity today, they often receive a direct answer — synthesised, confident, and sourced from a handful of trusted pages. Your website may rank on page one and still be invisible in that response. That's the problem GEO is designed to solve.

**Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO)** is the practice of structuring your website so that AI-powered search engines can understand, trust, and cite your content. Where traditional SEO focuses on getting pages to rank, GEO focuses on getting content to be *used* — pulled into AI Overviews, featured in chatbot responses, and quoted by the large language models that an increasing proportion of your potential audience now relies on for answers.

## Why it matters right now

AI search adoption is accelerating rapidly. Tools like Google's AI Overviews, Perplexity, and ChatGPT Search are reshaping how people find information — and they are highly selective about their sources. Pages that lack clear structure, authoritative signals, and machine-readable context are simply passed over, regardless of their traditional search ranking.

GEO isn't a replacement for SEO. It's the next layer. And the websites that get ahead of this shift now will hold a significant advantage as AI-mediated discovery becomes the norm.

## What a GEO audit covers

A proper GEO audit examines how well your site communicates with AI systems — from your structured data and metadata to your content architecture, entity signals, and crawlability. It identifies exactly where you're losing visibility and what to do about it.
